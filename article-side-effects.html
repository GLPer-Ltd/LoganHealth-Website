Managing Side Effects: A Practical Guide

Nausea, fatigue, or digestive changes? Most side effects are temporary. Here are proven strategies to minimise discomfort during your treatment.

5 Jan 2025 6 min read

Understanding GLP-1 Side Effects

The most common side effects of GLP-1 medications like Wegovy and Mounjaro are mild to moderate stomach issues including nausea, loose stools, or constipation. These symptoms typically emerge within days of starting treatment or increasing your dose and generally improve within 2-4 weeks as your body adjusts.

The good news? Most side effects are manageable with the right strategies, and they're a sign that the medication is working to slow digestion and reduce appetite. This guide will help you navigate common side effects with practical, evidence-based solutions.

Important to Know:

Side effects are typically most noticeable when starting treatment or increasing doses. They usually subside within 2-4 weeks as your body adapts. The gradual dosing schedule is specifically designed to minimise these effects.

Common Side Effects and Solutions

1. Nausea

Nausea is the most frequently reported side effect of GLP-1 medications. It occurs because the medication slows gastric emptying, meaning food stays in your stomach longer.

Proven Strategies to Reduce Nausea:

Start Low, Go Slow
  • Follow the prescribed titration schedule exactly
  • Don't rush to increase doses—give your body time to adjust
  • If nausea is severe, discuss staying at your current dose longer with your pharmacist
Adjust Your Eating Habits
  • Eat smaller, more frequent meals: 4-6 small meals work better than 3 large ones
  • Eat slowly: Take at least 20 minutes for each meal
  • Stop when satisfied: Don't force yourself to finish portions
  • Don't lie down after eating: Wait 2-3 hours before lying flat
  • Avoid late-night eating: Finish your last meal 3 hours before bed
Choose the Right Foods
  • Avoid trigger foods: Fatty, greasy, spicy foods and alcohol
  • Choose bland, easily digestible options: Toast, crackers, rice, bananas
  • Try ginger: Ginger tea, ginger biscuits, or ginger chews can help settle your stomach
  • Keep food simple: Plain grilled chicken, steamed vegetables, clear broths
  • Stay away from strong smells: Cook with windows open, avoid overpowering food odours
Optimise Injection Timing
  • Many people find injecting in the evening (before bed) helps them "sleep through" initial nausea
  • Experiment to find what works best for your schedule
  • Stay consistent with timing once you find what works — use the GLPer app's medication tracking to monitor your Wegovy or Mounjaro doses and stay on schedule
Stay Hydrated
  • Sip water throughout the day (8-10 glasses)
  • Try cold beverages—they're often easier to tolerate
  • Consider ice chips or frozen grapes if plain water is difficult
  • Herbal teas (especially ginger or peppermint) can be soothing

2. Constipation

Constipation occurs because GLP-1 medications slow the movement of food through your digestive system. Additionally, reduced food intake means less material moving through your bowels.

How to Prevent and Manage Constipation:

Increase Fibre Intake
  • Aim for 25-30 grams of fibre daily
  • Increase gradually to avoid bloating and gas
  • Choose high-fibre foods: vegetables, fruits, whole grains, legumes
  • Consider a fibre supplement (psyllium husk) if needed
Stay Well Hydrated
  • Drink at least 8-10 glasses of water daily
  • Warm liquids in the morning can stimulate bowel movements
  • Prune juice or prunes are natural laxatives
Get Moving
  • Regular physical activity helps stimulate digestion
  • Even a 15-20 minute daily walk can make a difference
  • Yoga poses that twist the torso can help
Consider Supplements
  • Magnesium supplements can help soften stools
  • Probiotics may support digestive health
  • Over-the-counter stool softeners if needed (consult your pharmacist first)

3. Diarrhoea

Some people experience diarrhoea instead of constipation, particularly when first starting treatment or after dose increases.

Managing Diarrhoea:

  • Stay hydrated: Replace lost fluids with water and electrolyte drinks
  • Follow the BRAT diet temporarily: Bananas, Rice, Applesauce, Toast
  • Avoid trigger foods: Dairy, caffeine, fatty foods, artificial sweeteners
  • Eat soluble fibre: Oats, bananas, sweet potatoes
  • Consider probiotics: May help restore gut balance
  • Monitor symptoms: If severe or persistent beyond 48 hours, contact your pharmacist

4. Fatigue

Temporary fatigue can occur as your body adjusts to eating less and processing food differently.

Combating Fatigue:

Ensure Adequate Nutrition
  • Meet your protein targets (1.2g per kg body weight)
  • Don't cut calories too drastically
  • Take a multivitamin to prevent deficiencies
  • Check iron and B12 levels if fatigue persists
Stay Hydrated
  • Dehydration causes fatigue
  • Aim for pale yellow urine as a hydration indicator
Optimise Sleep
  • Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep
  • Maintain consistent sleep/wake times
  • Avoid eating close to bedtime
Exercise Appropriately
  • Light to moderate exercise can boost energy
  • Don't overdo it—listen to your body
  • Even short walks can help

5. Bloating and Abdominal Discomfort

Feeling overly full or bloated is common as digestion slows.

Reducing Bloating:

  • Eat smaller portions more frequently
  • Chew food thoroughly and eat slowly
  • Avoid carbonated beverages
  • Limit gas-producing foods initially (beans, cruciferous vegetables, onions)
  • Try peppermint tea after meals
  • Take a short walk after eating to aid digestion
  • Avoid chewing gum (increases swallowed air)

6. Headaches

Headaches can occur, particularly in the first few weeks.

Managing Headaches:

  • Ensure adequate hydration (often the primary cause)
  • Don't skip meals—low blood sugar can trigger headaches
  • Manage stress through relaxation techniques
  • Get sufficient sleep
  • Paracetamol or ibuprofen if needed (follow dosing instructions)
  • Contact your pharmacist if headaches are severe or persistent

General Side Effect Management Strategies

Keep a Symptom Journal

Track when side effects occur, their severity, and what you ate or did beforehand. This helps identify patterns and triggers:

  • Time of day symptoms occur
  • Foods eaten in the 24 hours before
  • Activity levels
  • Hydration status
  • Days since injection

The GLPer app streamlines this process with dedicated side effects logging alongside your food diary and medication tracking, making it easy to spot connections between what you eat and how you feel.

Maintain Hydration

This cannot be overstated—proper hydration helps with nearly every side effect:

  • Reduces nausea
  • Prevents constipation
  • Combats fatigue
  • Reduces headaches
  • Helps with bloating

Target: 8-10 glasses (2-2.5 litres) daily, more if exercising or in hot weather.

Identify Personal Food Triggers

Everyone's triggers are slightly different. Common culprits include:

  • Fatty, greasy, or fried foods
  • Spicy dishes
  • Dairy products
  • Alcohol
  • Caffeine
  • Artificial sweeteners
  • High-fibre foods (initially)

Log your meals and symptoms in the GLPer app to quickly identify which foods trigger your side effects, helping you make informed choices about what to eat.

When to Contact Your Pharmacist:

Most side effects are manageable, but contact Logan Health immediately if you experience:

  • Severe, persistent vomiting preventing hydration
  • Severe abdominal pain (especially upper right abdomen)
  • Signs of pancreatitis (severe stomach pain radiating to back)
  • Signs of gallbladder problems (yellowing skin/eyes, fever with abdominal pain)
  • Allergic reactions (rash, swelling, breathing difficulties)
  • Vision changes
  • Rapid heartbeat or chest pain
  • Severe dehydration (dark urine, dizziness, confusion)

The Timeline: When Side Effects Improve

Timeframe What to Expect
Days 1-7 Side effects may begin, typically mild at starting dose
Weeks 2-3 Side effects may peak but body begins adapting
Week 4+ Most side effects significantly improved or resolved
After dose increases Mild return of symptoms for 1-2 weeks, then improvement

Long-Term Success Tips

Build Sustainable Habits

Use the medication as a tool to establish healthy patterns:

  • Regular meal times
  • Balanced, nutritious food choices — browse 500+ GLP-1 friendly recipes in the GLPer app
  • Consistent physical activity
  • Adequate hydration
  • Sufficient sleep
  • Stress management

Stay in Touch with Your Healthcare Team

  • Regular check-ins help optimise your treatment
  • Don't hesitate to discuss side effects—they can adjust your plan
  • Some people benefit from staying at a lower maintenance dose
  • Your pharmacist can provide personalised strategies

Connect with Others

  • Join support groups (online or in-person)
  • Share experiences and tips with others on the same journey
  • Learn from those who've successfully managed side effects

Remember:

Side effects are typically temporary and manageable. Most people find that by week 3-4, symptoms have significantly improved, and the benefits of appetite reduction and weight loss far outweigh the initial discomfort.
